$hypot



The $hypot identifier returns the hypotenuse for the other 2 sides of a right triangle.

Synopsis

$hypot(,)
== Switches == None

Parameters

* '''A''' - One of the shorter sides of the right triangle * '''B''' - The other of the shorter sides of the right triangle Doesn't matter which order the short sides are given. You can use the letter 'e' or 'd' as *10^N, $hypot(5e6,1) = $hypot($calc(5*10^6),1)

Properties

None

Example

//var %a 3 | var %b 4 | echo -a $hypot(%a,%b) is the same as $sqrt( $calc( %a ^2 + %b ^2 ) )

Compatibility

Added: mIRC v7.33 (27 May 2014)
