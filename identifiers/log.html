$log



The $log identifier returns the natural (base e) logarithm of a number. You are limited to 6 numbers after the decimal by default. You can use /bigfloat and get 30 numbers after the decimal.

Synopsis

$log(N)

Parameters

N - The number you want the natural logarithm of.

Properties

None

Example

Returns: 2.302585 
//echo -a $log(10)
Use bigfloat:
Returns: 2.302585092994045684017991454684 
//bigfloat on | echo -a $log(10)
You can use $log to find the base-X logarithm for any value N:
Returns: 3.560878 
//echo -a base-3 logrithm of 50 is $calc($log(50) / $log(3))
You can use $round():
Returns: 8.000001 -- Rounded: 8 
//echo -a $calc( $log(256) / $log(2) ) -- Rounded: $round($calc( $log(256) / $log(2)),0)

Compatibility

Added: mIRC v5.3 (13 Dec 1997)

Notes

There may be a rounding error due to mIRC preserving fractional digits.
