$log10



The $log10 identifier returns the common (base 10) logarithm of a number. You are limited to 6 numbers after the decimal by default. You can use /bigfloat to get 30 numbers after the decimal.

Synopsis

$log10(N)

Parameters

N - The number you want the common (base 10) logarithm of.

Properties

None

Example

Returns: 1.69897 
//echo -a $log10(50)
Use bigfloat:
Returns: 1.698970004336018804786261105276 
//bigfloat on | echo -a $log10(50)

Compatibility

Added: mIRC v7.33 (27 May 2014)

Notes

There may be a rounding error due to mIRC preserving fractional digits. - See $log for more examples.
