$log2



The $log2 identifier returns the common (base 2) logarithm of a number. You are limited to 6 numbers after the decimal by default. You can use /bigfloat to get 30 numbers after the decimal.

Synopsis

$log2(N)

Parameters

N - The number you want the common (base 2) logarithm of.

Properties

None

Example

Returns: 2.807355 
//echo -a $log2(7)
Use bigfloat:
Returns: 2.807354922057604067832435557955 
//bigfloat on | echo -a $log2(7)

Compatibility

Added: mIRC v7.72 (27 Nov 2022)

Notes

There may be a rounding error due to mIRC preserving fractional digits. - See $log for more examples.
